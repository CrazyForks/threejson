# ThreeJSON Project Trademark and Brand Policy

[简体中文](./TRADEMARK.zh-CN.md)

**Version 1.0 — Last updated: 2026-07-11**

## 1. Purpose

ThreeJSON is an open-source project. Source code and other materials that are expressly licensed under the MIT License may be used, copied, modified, distributed, sublicensed, and commercially used subject to that license.

This Trademark and Brand Policy applies only to the names and brand assets used to identify the official ThreeJSON Project and its official products.

**This policy does not modify, replace, or restrict any permission granted by the MIT License or by another license expressly applied to particular files or materials.**

## 2. Definitions

For purposes of this policy:

- **“ThreeJSON Project”** means the official ThreeJSON project and the person or entity publicly identified through the official website or official repository as the holder or authorized steward of the relevant brand rights.
- **“Official Project Channels”** means the official website, repositories, organizations, package-publisher accounts, and other accounts linked or identified through the official ThreeJSON website or official repository.
- **“Brand Assets”** means official logos, wordmarks, logo lockups, and brand-specific icons used primarily to identify the ThreeJSON Project or one of its official products.

## 3. Official Names Covered by This Policy

The official project and product identifiers currently covered by this policy include:

- ThreeJSON
- ThreeBox
- ThreeJSON Core
- ThreeJSON Domain
- ThreeJSON SceneEditor
- ThreeJSON ScenePlayer
- ThreeJSON SceneShower
- ThreeBox Community

Other names are covered by this policy only to the extent that they are publicly used or expressly designated as official names through the Official Project Channels.

This policy does not claim exclusive rights over ordinary or generic words used independently, including:

- core;
- domain;
- plugin;
- editor;
- player;
- scene;
- shower;
- community;
- cloud;
- pro;
- plus;
- ultimate.

In particular, this policy does not restrict the ordinary use of the word “domain” or the creation of custom Domains through ThreeJSON’s extension APIs.

This policy applies only to branding and official identity. It does not impose additional restrictions on code or other materials that are expressly distributed under an open-source or open-content license.

Third-party assets retain their applicable licenses, attribution requirements, and other notices.

## 6. Descriptive and Community Use

You may use the names ThreeJSON and ThreeBox without prior permission when making accurate, non-misleading statements such as:

- Built with ThreeJSON
- Powered by ThreeJSON
- Uses ThreeJSON
- Compatible with ThreeJSON
- Supports ThreeJSON
- Based on ThreeJSON
- Forked from ThreeJSON

You may also use the names to:

- link to the official website or repository;
- discuss, review, teach, compare, or report on the projects;
- identify compatibility or interoperability;
- accurately describe a relationship with the official projects;
- identify the upstream project from which a fork or derivative work was created.

Such use must not imply that a third-party project is official, endorsed, sponsored, certified, maintained, approved, or otherwise affiliated with the ThreeJSON Project when that relationship does not exist.

## 7. Domains, Plugins, and Other Extensions

ThreeJSON is designed to be extended.

Developers may create, distribute, and commercially use their own:

- Domains;
- Plugins;
- Components;
- Scripts;
- Runtime extensions;
- adapters;
- AI integrations;
- tools;
- other integrations built with the public ThreeJSON APIs.

This policy does not claim ownership of third-party Domains, Plugins, integrations, or other extensions. Their ownership and licensing are determined by their authors, contributors, employers, contracts, and any applicable licenses or other agreements.

Users may freely define, register, distribute, and commercially use custom ThreeJSON Domains, subject to the applicable software and asset licenses.

Third-party projects are encouraged to use names that clearly identify their third-party source, for example:

- Acme Factory Domain for ThreeJSON
- Acme ThreeJSON Adapter
- `@acme/threejson-plugin`
- `acme-threejson-domain`

A third party may use ThreeJSON or ThreeBox descriptively in a project or package name when its independent source is clear and the overall presentation is not likely to imply official status.

Names such as the following require prior written permission when their use is likely to appear official:

- ThreeJSON Official Factory Plugin
- Official ThreeBox Extension
- ThreeJSON Core
- ThreeJSON SceneEditor

The restriction in this paragraph concerns misleading official identity, not compatibility or ordinary descriptive use.

## 8. Forks and Modified Distributions

Forking and modification are permitted under the applicable open-source license.

A repository visibly identified by its hosting platform as a fork may retain the repository name generated or permitted by that platform.

When a modified version is independently published, promoted, or distributed:

- it must not be presented as an official ThreeJSON or ThreeBox release;
- material modifications should be disclosed clearly;
- the identity of the third-party maintainer or distributor should be reasonably clear.

A modified distribution must not use ThreeJSON or ThreeBox as its primary branding when the overall presentation is likely to confuse users about whether it is an official release.

Using a distinct primary name is strongly recommended for independently maintained or materially modified distributions. The relationship may still be described accurately, for example:

- Forked from ThreeJSON
- Based on ThreeJSON
- Powered by ThreeJSON

## 9. Repositories, Packages, Domains, Organizations, and Services

The names ThreeJSON and ThreeBox identify the official projects maintained through the Official Project Channels.

Without prior written permission, you may not use ThreeJSON, ThreeBox, an official product name, or a confusingly similar name in a way that is likely to make users reasonably believe that a third-party repository, package, distribution, website, service, product, company, organization, or account is:

- an official project or release;
- maintained or published by the ThreeJSON Project;
- endorsed, sponsored, certified, approved, or supported by the ThreeJSON Project;
- otherwise officially affiliated with the ThreeJSON Project.

This principle applies to potentially misleading use in:

- GitHub repositories and organizations;
- npm and other package registries;
- domain names and websites;
- application and service names;
- downloadable distributions;
- company and organization names;
- social-media and community accounts;
- cloud and hosted services.

This section does not prohibit accurate descriptive uses such as `@acme/threejson-adapter`, “Compatible with ThreeJSON,” or “Unofficial community tool for ThreeJSON,” where the independent third-party status is clear.

## 10. Uses Requiring Prior Permission

Prior written permission is required to:

- present a third-party project, product, or service as an official ThreeJSON or ThreeBox offering;
- use an official Brand Asset as the primary logo or identity of a third-party product, company, organization, or service;
- use official branding in a way that is likely to imply endorsement, sponsorship, certification, partnership, or official affiliation where none exists;
- register or operate a trademark, business name, domain, package, repository, organization, or account whose name and presentation are likely to be confused with an official ThreeJSON or ThreeBox identity;
- distribute a modified version while presenting it as an official release;
- use official Brand Assets on merchandise or promotional materials in a way that is likely to imply official origin or endorsement.

## 11. Brand Assets

Unless expressly licensed otherwise, the MIT License does not grant permission to use official Brand Assets as the identity of a third-party product, service, company, organization, or project.

You may use official Brand Assets for truthful editorial, educational, documentary, comparative, or referential purposes when:

- the use is reasonably necessary to identify the project;
- the asset is not used as the third party’s own logo or primary identity;
- the presentation does not imply a relationship that does not exist.

Screenshots of the software, website, examples, or rendered scenes are not automatically treated as Brand Assets. Their use is governed by the applicable asset license and must not falsely imply official affiliation.

## 12. Official Status and Impersonation

Only repositories, packages, websites, accounts, releases, products, and services linked or identified through the Official Project Channels should be treated as official.

The mere appearance of ThreeJSON, ThreeBox, or a related term in a repository, package, domain, organization, or account name does not make that item official.

No person may use the official names or Brand Assets to impersonate the ThreeJSON Project, misrepresent the source of software or services, redirect users to deceptive downloads, or otherwise mislead users about origin, maintenance, security, endorsement, or affiliation.

## 13. Good-Faith Resolution

The ThreeJSON Project intends this policy to protect users from impersonation and confusion, not to discourage legitimate open-source use, interoperability, community extensions, commentary, or commercial adoption.

When practical, apparent good-faith issues will ordinarily be addressed first by requesting reasonable clarification, attribution, renaming, or other corrective action.

Immediate or stronger action may be appropriate in cases involving fraud, deliberate impersonation, malware, deceptive distribution, security risks, repeated violations, or serious user confusion.

## 14. Policy Updates

This policy may be updated as the project and its governance evolve.

Material changes will be published through the Official Project Channels. Except where prompt action is reasonably necessary to prevent fraud, impersonation, security harm, or serious user confusion, users whose existing use complied with the previous policy will be given a reasonable period to adjust to a material change.

Failure to enforce this policy in one instance does not waive the ability to enforce it in another instance.
